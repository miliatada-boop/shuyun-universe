01 — Brand Philosophy·品牌哲学·Philosophie de Marque

Shuyun Universe exists at the intersection of freedom and depth. It is not a brand that sells wellness — it embodies a way of being: rooted in consciousness, moving without borders, gathering without noise.

The name holds its own cosmology. 舒 — to unfurl, to ease into. 云 — cloud, the formless that holds form. Universe — the container of all possibility. Together: a civilization that lives lightly but thinks deeply.

"We do not escape the world. We move through it more consciously."
「我们不是逃离世界，而是以更清醒的方式穿行其中。」
« Nous n'échappons pas au monde. Nous le traversons avec plus de conscience. »

Logo Construction Rules

  • The mark (two overlapping circles with inner ellipse) represents the intersection of worlds — cloud + cosmos + self
  • SHUYUN is set in Cormorant Garamond Light, tracked at +180 — never compressed, never bolded
  • Universe is set in DM Sans Light, tracked at +550 — the contrast between serif and sans is intentional
  • Minimum clear space = cap height of the wordmark on all sides
  • Minimum size: 80px wide digital / 20mm print — never smaller
  • Approved backgrounds: Abyssal (#1C2B35), Chalk (#F5F2EE), Sand Veil (#E8E2D8) only
  • Never: drop shadows, outlines, gradient fills, rotation, or color variations outside the palette

Digital Usage

Hero backgrounds: Abyssal only. Page backgrounds: Chalk. Section dividers: Sand Veil. Never use gradient overlays — depth comes from opacity, not color mixing.

Print Usage

Paper stock should read Chalk or warmer. Abyssal prints as deep navy — acceptable. Mist and Warm Drift as Pantone 7543 C and 7527 C respectively.

Photography Toning

Edit toward the Mist and Deep Current range. Reduce saturation, lift shadows slightly, cool highlights. The palette should feel as if filtered through ocean light.

What to Avoid

Pure white (#FFFFFF) feels clinical. Pure black (#000000) is too harsh. Saturated colors, warm yellows, or social-media brights break the atmosphere entirely.

Gold Usage Rules

Antique Gold appears as hairline rules, seal outlines, and single typographic accents — never as fill or large background. Max one gold element per layout. Pale Gold can appear as a very subtle surface warm on packaging only.

Lacquer Usage Rules

Lacquer and Cinnabar are reserved for physical materials and ceremonies: wax seals, gathering invitations, cover edition rules, and the inner spine of the printed journal. Never used on the website or social media as a color block.

06 — Photography Direction·摄影方向·Direction Photographique
01
Light as Atmosphere
Shoot in diffused, directional light only. No direct sun. Prefer overcast Nordic skies, blue hour, or interior light from large windows. Highlights should breathe — never blown. Shadows should hold detail. Think: a photograph taken through slightly fogged glass.
02
Human Presence Without Performance
People appear as atmosphere, not subject. They are reading, looking away, in conversation, in motion. Never posed. Never looking at camera. Never performing happiness. The feeling: you have stepped into someone's real life, not their curated moment.
03
Negative Space as Meaning
Compositions favor breathing room. A cup on a window sill. A hand resting on a book. One object on a vast surface. The emptiness is not empty — it carries the weight of the unsaid. Ratio: minimum 40% negative space in hero images.
04
Geography as Character
Locations should feel borderless — recognizable as a state of being, not a country. Coast. Mountain. Old city. New interior. The recurring thread: places where time moves differently. Architecture that has been lived in. Nature that is indifferent to the viewer.
05
Post-Processing Signature
Desaturate to 60–70% of original. Lift shadows by +15–20. Cool highlights toward blue-grey. Add subtle grain (15–25%) for materiality. Reduce contrast slightly — the world should feel soft at the edges. Final tone: always warmer in shadows, cooler in highlights.

Photograph These

  • Hands around a ceramic bowl in morning light
  • A coastline at blue hour, no horizon line cliché
  • Interior: single light source, objects mid-use
  • Two people in conversation, faces soft-focus
  • Open book, no face. Hands, not face.
  • Architecture: doorways, passages, thresholds
  • Natural materials: stone, water, linen, wood

Avoid These

  • Staged flat-lays (unless for packaging only)
  • High-saturation nature shots (Instagram travel)
  • People smiling directly at camera
  • Yoga poses or meditation clichés
  • Golden hour lens flare
  • Stock photo "diversity" compositions
  • Motivational quote overlaid on landscape

  • Annual · rotating location
  • Application-based access

The Shuyun Gathering is not a retreat in the conventional sense. There are no fixed programs, no speakers with slides, no morning routines imposed on everyone. It is a curated context — the right people, the right place, the right amount of structure — within which something important can happen.

Mornings belong to individuals. Afternoons are for conversations that began at dinner and continued. Evenings are for sitting with what emerged.

Essay
We do not talk about wellness here. The word has become so saturated with commerce, so distorted by the industry that emerged around it, that it no longer points to anything real.

What we talk about instead is condition. The condition you are in when you are most yourself. When you are not performing, not optimizing, not documenting. Just present — in the full weight of that word.

The question Shuyun Universe asks is simple: what creates that condition? For some it is solitude and sea. For others it is the right table at the right hour with the right two people. For most, it is some version of both.

We are in the business of creating conditions. Not outcomes. Not transformations. Just the quality of space and gathering that allows people to meet themselves more fully.
Grid System
Margins
Print: 20mm all sides. Digital: 80px desktop / 24px mobile. Inner margin always wider than outer.
Columns
Editorial: 2 columns for body, 1 column for full-width display. Never 3 columns — too dense for this brand.
Image / Text Ratio
Print: 60% image, 40% text. Digital: flexible, but never less than 30% white space in any section.
